Fun Fact!

When Ernő Rubik invented his cube in 1974, he didn't actually know how to solve it. With six coloured sides, 21 pieces and 54 outer surfaces, there are over 43 quintillion (that's 43,252,003,274,489,856,000 to be precise) different possible configurations.
